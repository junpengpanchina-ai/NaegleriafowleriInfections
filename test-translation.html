Naegleria fowleri Infection

Naegleria fowleri is a free-living amoeba, a kind of one-celled organism that thrives in warm freshwater lakes, rivers, and hot springs.

Symptoms and Diagnosis

Initial symptoms of PAM (Primary Amebic Meningoencephalitis) start about 5 days after infection and may include:

  • Headache
  • Fever
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Stiff neck

Prevention

You can reduce your risk by following these prevention tips:

  • Avoid jumping or diving into warm freshwater
  • Hold your nose shut or use nose clips when jumping or diving
